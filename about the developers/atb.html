Overview

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD) is childhood's most common neurodevelopmental disorder. It includes persistent problems, such as difficulty sustaining attention, hyperactivity, impulsive behavior, and poor academic progress. Most children with ADHD struggle with low self-esteem, troubled-relationship, and impulsive behavior. According to the ADHD Society of the Philippines (2016), an estimated 3 to 5 percent of the population aged 0-14 years old suffers from this mental disorder.In the recent study of Hassan (2009), the cognitive skills and ability of ADHD are a form of the basis of social sufficiency behavior.One of the leading leisure activities for children is playing computer games. According to Dopfner (2012), there are existing developmental benefits or positive effects of computer games used for educational and therapeutic purposes. Children with ADHD found interest or valuable within. The researcher believes that there is an evolution of technology that needs to take advantage of developing applications to enhance and stimulate the learning process of children with ADHD.
